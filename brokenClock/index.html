Broken Clock
INTRO
Even a broken clock is right twice a day.

Or is it? This aphorism is used to say something about the state of rightness by noting that even a broken thing can be right in some sense.

But can a clock be broken in a way that it is right more or less than 2 times a day?

REFLECT

After messing around for a while, you will probably step away with a few observations. I will provide a way to share your observations in the future. If you know me IRL, please share!

Here are a few points I have heard noted:
analog broken clocks have a restricted form of broken. A digital clock can transition from fast broke to slow broke without the possibility of being right in the middle.
a clock can be broken, but more right than other broken clocks, e.g., 
return thisMinute + 1;
versus 
return 33;
most clocks we use are broken in the sense that they are never exactly right on some level, e.g., 
return thisMinute + 0.00001;
there are a ton of seconds in a day, much more opportunity to be broken, e.g., 
0.0000000000001
